ENU

Gumilyov Eurasian National University (ENU; Kazakh Gumilyov atyndagy Eurasia Ulttyk University) is a higher educational institution in Nur-Sultan. Named in honor of the Russian scientist, historian and ethnologist L.N. Gumilyov. ENU includes 28 scientific institutions (research institutes, laboratories, centers), 13 faculties, the Institute of Continuing Education and Advanced Training, a military department, cultural and educational centers of various states. The system of training specialists at the Eurasian National University is conducted at 3 levels of education: higher basic education (bachelor's degree), master's and doctoral studies. The university prepares specialists in 65 specialties of bachelor's degree, 68 - magistracy and 38 - doctoral studies.

History

The L.N. Gumilyov Eurasian National University was founded on May 23, 1996 at the initiative of the President of the Republic of Kazakhstan N.A. Nazarbayev on the basis of the merger of two institutes - the Tselinograd Civil Engineering Institute and the Tselinograd Pedagogical Institute. By the decree of the President of the Republic of Kazakhstan dated July 5, 2001, the university was assigned a special national status, "taking into account the significant contribution to the formation, development and professional development of an individual on the basis of national and universal values, achievements of science and technology" [1]. The university was named after Lev Nikolaevich Gumilyov. [2] In 2000, by the Decree of the Government of the Republic of Kazakhstan, the L.N. Gumilyov Eurasian National University by merging with the Diplomatic Academy of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Kazakhstan was transformed into the L.N. Gumilyov Eurasian State University (YSU). In 2000, on the basis of the Eurasian National University, the Kazakhstan branch of the Lomonosov Moscow State University was opened. [3] In 2001, the university was again renamed into the L. N. Gumilyov Eurasian National University [4]. On October 1, 2002, on the initiative of the rector M. Zh. Zholdasbekov, the Museum-Study of L. N. Gumilyov was opened at the L. N. Gumilyov ENU. [5] In September 2008, a bust of a Eurasian was installed in the ENU educational and administrative building.

KAZGUU

KAZGUU University named after M.S. Narikbayeva (earlier - Kazak Humanitarian Law University) (Kazakh M.S. Narikbaev atyndaǵy KAZGUU Universiteti), (eng. M. Narikbayev KAZGUU University) is a higher educational institution located in Nur-Sultan and created in 1994. There are 4 schools at the university - the Higher School for the Humanities (HSH), the Graduate School of Law (HSL), the Higher School of Economics (HSE) and the Business School. [1] Narikbayev Talgat Maksutovich - Chairman of the Board of the KAZGUU University named after M.S. Narikbayev Every year, the Atameken National Chamber of Entrepreneurs, together with the Ministry of Education and Science of the Republic of Kazakhstan, conducts a study of educational programs of domestic universities and compiles a rating based on them. The main goal of the rating is to provide complete and reliable information to schoolchildren on the professions in demand, to indicate which universities provide high-quality training for them and where there are more chances of finding a job. In December 2020. KAZGUU University named after M.S. Narikbayev was awarded the first place in the Top-10 universities according to the rating of educational programs "Atameken". In 2019. The university ranked second.

history

KAZGUU University was founded in 1994 in accordance with the Decree of the President of Kazakhstan N. A. Nazarbayev. On November 5, 1996, KazHYuI was transformed into the Kazakh State Law University by the Decree of the Government of Kazakhstan and transferred from the jurisdiction of the Ministry of Justice to the Ministry of Education and Science [2]. Since 1997, the journal "Law and State" has been published. In 2000, the Kazakh State Law University was merged with the Institute of State and Law of the National Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Kazakhstan, and on February 14, 2000, the Kazakh State Law Academy (KazHLA) was established. On October 24, 2001, the Republican State Treasury Enterprise "KazGUA" was transformed into CJSC "Kazakh Humanitarian and Legal University" (KazGUU) with a location in the city of Nur-Sultan. The head university moved to Nur-Sultan in 2002 (a branch of KazGUU in Astana was established in the 1998-1999 academic year). On January 16, 2003, KazGUU CJSC was transformed into KazGUU OJSC, and then on May 19, 2004 - into KazGUU JSC. In September 2007, KazGUU signed the Great Charter of Universities in Bologna. July 2, 2008 with the participation of the President of the country N.A. Nazarbayev, a solemn ceremony of opening a new educational building of JSC "KazHUU" with a total area of ​​32,235.1 m2, located on the left bank of the Yesil River, along Korgalzhinskoe shosse street 8, took place. In 2013, on the eve of the 20th anniversary of the university, the Development Strategy "2013-2020 - a world-class university" was adopted. On February 10, 2015, the educational institution is undergoing state re-registration of the Joint Stock Company "KazGUU University" (JSC "KazGUU University"

Achievements

On January 3, 2013, the Independent Kazakhstani Agency for Quality Assurance in Education (certificate of the Ministry of Education and Science of the Republic of Kazakhstan dated June 27, 2012 No. BFM-001) issued a Certificate of Institutional Accreditation to JSC "KazGUU" [4]. On June 30, 2016, the programs of the Higher School of Economics "Bachelor of Finance" and "Bachelor of Accounting and Auditing" are accredited by the ACCA (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants). On September 30, 2016, KazGUU University passed the international accreditation FIBAA (Foundation for International Accreditation of Programs in the Field of Business Administration) [5]. In April 2017, the Higher School of Economics received accreditation under the ICAEW (Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales) certification program. The only and first case in Kazakhstan when a university received ACA ICAEW accreditation in 7 modules: Accounting, Assurance, Business and Finance, Law, Management Information, Principles of Taxation, Financial Management. On July 21, 2017, the specialties "Accounting and Auditing", "Finance", "Economics" and "Management" of the Higher School of Economics of KAZGUU University received academic recognition and were accredited by CIMA (Chartered Institute of Management Accountants). In December 2020. KAZGUU University named after M.S. Narikbayev took first place in the rating of educational programs of universities of the National Chamber of Entrepreneurs of the Republic of Kazakhstan "Atameken"

ALMAU

Almaty Management University (Kazakh Almaty Management University, English Almaty Management University, AlmaU) is a higher educational institution in Alma-Ata that trains specialists for undergraduate, graduate, doctoral, MBA (Master of Business Administration) and DBA (Doctor of Business Administration) programs. ). AlmaU is one of the first business universities in Kazakhstan. The educational institution was organized in 1988 as the Almaty School of Managers, in 1996 it was transformed into the International Academy of Business. After in 2014, MAB was awarded the status of the University - Almaty Management University (AlmaU)

History

In the fall of 1988, the Almaty City Executive Committee initiated the creation of the republican cooperative union "Union", the head of which was invited Leonid Solomin. Together with Bayangali Koishibaev, he planned to create the first business school in Kazakhstan [2]. On December 12, 1988, in Alma-Ata, on the basis of the Soyuz cooperative association, the Alma-Ata School of Managers (AShM) was organized, its first students were engaged in the assembly hall of the Soviet District Party Committee for exactly one month [2]. In the first period of the existence of the educational institution, training was carried out in the form of short-term courses, which, as a rule, were attended by people with higher education [3] [4]. In October 1989, Asylbek Kozhakhmetov became the director of the school, who had previously worked at the ASM as a computer science teacher [2]. In 1990, the ASM team took part in the organization of the Association of International Business Schools, the director of the ASM Asylbek Kozhakhmetov was a member of the council of the association, the assembly of institutes, schools for training entrepreneurs of the CIS [3] [4]. In 1992, the school began work on the creation of an MBA (Master of Business Administration) program and the development of a project for the implementation of programs for management personnel [3] [5] [6], ASM was the first in Kazakhstan to introduce MBA to the country's educational market [7]. In 1994, the Ministry of Economy of Kazakhstan announced a tender for an enterprise consulting project funded by the World Bank. Of the six Western organizations participating in the tender, five (European Foundation for Management Development, Lyon School of Business, Maastricht School of Management, and others) named ASM as the most preferred partner [8]. In 1995, the educational institution was renamed into the Almaty School of Management [7]. In October 1995, the American organization People to People International awarded the ASM the Birmingham Torch honorary award for successful economic survival and development in an emerging market economy [9] [10] [11]. In 1996, ASM held an international conference "Human Resource Management: Strategy and Practice" with the participation of more than 100 delegates from 15 countries of the world, Minister of Economy Umirzak Shukeyev, Adviser to the President of Kazakhstan on Economic Affairs Altai Tleuberdin, First Secretary of the European Union Delegation to Kazakhstan Onno Simons, UN Resident Representative in Kazakhstan Nigel Ringrose and others
